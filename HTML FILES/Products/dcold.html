INTRODUCTION


D Cold Total Tablet is an Analgesic and Antipyretic, Stimulant and Decongestants used for the relief of cold symptoms including blocked nose, headache, sinus pain, body pains, sore throat and chills. It contains paracetamol, caffeine and phenylephrine as active ingredients. Paracetamol acts as an analgesic, and antipyretic, which works by inhibiting the production of a chemical called prostaglandins which are responsible for pain and fever. Caffeine is a stimulant which helps to increase brain activity and improves alertness. Phenylephrine works by decreasing swelling in the nose and ears. Thus, helps to breathe easier. Take D Cold Total Tablet as advised by your doctor. Swallow the tablet with water. Do not crush or chew the tablet. Take one tablet every 4-6 hours. Do not exceed 4 tablets in any 24 hour period. It is not recommended for use in children under 12 years of age.

SIDE EFFECTS OF D COLD TOTAL TABLET


D Cold Total Tablet may cause allergic reactions like itching, skin rash or peeling, mouth ulceration, swelling of the face, mouth and throat causing difficulty breathing and unexplained bruising or bleeding, loss of vision, unusually fast or irregular heartbeat, difficulty in passing water in some individuals. Discontinue and consult your doctor if you notice any such side effects while taking D Cold Total Tablet.

SAFETY ADVICE


  • Always take D Cold Total Tablet as advised by your doctor
  • Take one tablet every 4-6 hours. Do not exceed 4 tablets in any 24 hour period
  • Swallow the tablet with water. Do not crush or chew the tablet
  • Do not take D Cold Total Tablet if you are allergic to any of its ingredients
  • It is not recommended for use in children under 12 years of age
  • Do not use this medicine if you are a pregnant or lactating woman unless recommended by the doctor
  • Do not use if you are suffering from severe heart diseases, high blood pressure, diabetes or hypothyroidism
  • Inform your doctor if you are suffering from kidney disease, liver disease, prostate disease and peptic ulcer
  • Inform your doctor about all the medicines you use, including prescription, over-the-counter medications, and herbal supplements
